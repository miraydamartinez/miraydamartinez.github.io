about me

I am a Houston native currently pursuing a Bachelor of Arts at Swarthmore College. As a third-year Computer Science and Economics student, I am passionate about using technology to build tools that help increase access to services for low-income communities.

This summer, I am working as a research assistant for Stephen Golub, a professor in the Economics Department at Swarthmore College. I am conducting research and drafting a literature review on various kinship groups in Africa and their role in cross-border trade. Through this experience, I have developed a better understanding of the importance of trust and networks in successful economic transactions.

In addition, I recently joined the Codecademy team as a code coach and occasional project reviewer. In this role, I assist 300+ individuals in defining goals and clarifying programming concepts so that they may be better equipped for their jobs and classes. I specialize in Python, but also assist those learning Git, the Command Line, HTML, CSS, and Javascript.

My interests include the intersection of technology and economic development and social innovation and entrepreneurship.
