about me

I am a rising junior at Swarthmore College pursuing a double major in Economics and Computer Science. During the 2016-2017 academic year, I had the opportunity to work as the Assistant Manager for the Swarthmore Student Budget Committee (SBC), which allocates approximately $600,000 in funds to student groups. My role was to support the current Manager and assist with student reimbursements, cash advances, and service payments. I was promoted to Manager at the end of my term and served in that role for all of fall 2017. As Manager, I led and interacted constantly with treasurers and administration members by disseminating important information, facilitating interactions, and always being available.

Following my position as Manager, I completed a week-long software engineering externship at Salesforce, an American cloud computing company that specializes as a Customer Relationship Management (CRM) platform. During my externship, I gained familiarity with Kubernetes, an open-source system used to automate deployment, scale, and manage containerized applications.

This summer, I am working as research assistant for Stephen Golub, a professor in the Economics Department at Swarthmore College. I am focusing on the role of kinship groups in cross-border trade in Africa. I am also working as a code coach / advisor for Codecademy, where I provide students with 1:1 technical assistance in Python, Javascript, HTML, and CSS.

I share an interest for social entrepreneurship, startups, machine learning, and deep learning and am currently looking to pursue opportunities at the intersection of computer science and economics.
